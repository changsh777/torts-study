Torts Essay Practice — IRAC 3문제

쟁점 포착 + IRAC 구조 + 모범 답안 개요 | 2026 UDSL
Essay 1 — Negligence (과실 종합)
Fact Pattern
Fred owns a chemical plant located next to a residential neighborhood. A city ordinance requires all chemical plant operators to inspect and maintain pipes on a quarterly basis. Fred last inspected the pipes two years ago. One night, a pipe burst and released toxic fumes into the neighborhood. Sarah, a neighbor, was exposed to the fumes. Unknown to Fred, Sarah had a pre-existing respiratory condition. As a result of the exposure, Sarah suffered severe and permanent lung damage far exceeding what a healthy person would have suffered. Bob, Sarah's friend who happened to be visiting her house that evening, also inhaled the fumes and suffered minor throat irritation. Fred argues that he could not have foreseen that anyone with a respiratory condition lived nearby.
Question: Discuss all negligence claims that Sarah and Bob may have against Fred. Address any defenses Fred may raise.
⚡ 쟁점 목록 (Issue Spotting)
① Negligence per se — 조례 위반 → breach 자동 성립 여부
② Eggshell Plaintiff Rule — Sarah의 기저질환, Fred가 몰랐어도 전액 책임 여부
③ Proximate Cause (foreseeability) — Bob은 foreseeable plaintiff인가?
④ Damages — Sarah(심각) vs Bob(경미) 비교
Issue 1: Negligence per se (Sarah v. Fred)
I — Issue
Did Fred's failure to maintain the pipes constitute negligence per se under the city ordinance?
R — Rule
Negligence per se applies when: (1) the defendant violated a statute/ordinance, (2) the statute was designed to prevent the type of harm that occurred, and (3) plaintiff was in the class of persons the statute was designed to protect. If established, breach is conclusively presumed.
A — Application
Fred violated the quarterly inspection ordinance (last inspection 2 years ago). The ordinance was designed to prevent toxic leaks harming nearby residents — exactly the harm that occurred. Sarah and Bob are residents/visitors = protected class.
C — Conclusion
Fred is negligent per se as to breach. Causation and damages still must be shown.
📝 한국어 요점
조례 위반 → breach 자동. "몰랐다"는 항변 불가. 조례 목적(독성 누출 방지) = 실제 피해와 일치 → negligence per se 성립.
Issue 2: Eggshell Plaintiff Rule (Sarah's Damages)
I
Is Fred liable for the full extent of Sarah's lung damage even though her pre-existing condition was unforeseeable?
R
Under the eggshell plaintiff rule, a defendant takes the plaintiff as they find them. The defendant is liable for the full extent of damages caused, even if the plaintiff's special vulnerability was unforeseeable.
A
Fred did not know of Sarah's respiratory condition. However, once Fred's negligence caused Sarah harm, he is responsible for all resulting damages. The foreseeability of the extent of harm is irrelevant — only the type of harm (injury from toxic exposure) must be foreseeable.
C
Fred is liable for the full extent of Sarah's permanent lung damage.
📝 한국어 요점
Fred의 항변: "기저질환 몰랐다" → 무효. Eggshell rule: 피해 정도 예견 불필요. 피해 유형(독성 노출)만 foreseeable이면 충분. 전액 배상.
Issue 3: Bob's Claim — Proximate Cause / Foreseeable Plaintiff
I
Was Bob a foreseeable plaintiff such that Fred owed him a duty of care?
R
Under the Palsgraf rule (majority), defendant owes a duty only to foreseeable plaintiffs within the zone of danger. A visitor in a neighboring house during a toxic release is generally foreseeable. Minority (Andrews): duty owed to all.
A
Bob was physically present at a neighboring residence when the fumes spread. A visitor at an adjacent house is within the foreseeable zone of danger of a large-scale toxic release from a nearby plant. Fred owed Bob a duty.
C
Bob may recover for his minor throat irritation as actual damages.
📝 한국어 요점
Bob = 인접 주택 방문자 → 독성 누출 위험 구역 내 foreseeable plaintiff. 피해 경미하나 actual damages 존재 → 청구 가능.
Essay 2 — Products Liability (제조물 책임)
Fact Pattern
LadderCo manufactures aluminum ladders. It sold a batch of ladders to HomeStore, a retailer. Alice purchased one of the ladders from HomeStore. Unknown to Alice, this particular ladder was assembled at the factory with rungs spaced 18 inches apart instead of the standard 12 inches — an error caused by a malfunctioning assembly machine. LadderCo's quality control team had noted complaints about rung spacing in prior models but never issued a warning or recalled the product. While Alice was using the ladder to clean gutters, it gave way because of the rung defect, and she fell, suffering a broken arm. Her contractor Tom, who was standing below to stabilize the base of the ladder, was struck by a falling rung and suffered a head laceration. Alice sues LadderCo. Tom sues LadderCo and HomeStore.
Question: Discuss the strict products liability and negligence claims. Address who may sue whom and what defects are at issue.
⚡ 쟁점 목록
① Manufacturing defect (제조 결함) — 표준 12인치 vs 실제 18인치
② Design defect (설계 결함) — 대안 설계 존재 여부 (risk-utility test)
③ Failure to warn (경고 결함) — 불만 인지 후 미경고
④ Bystander (Tom) — strict liability에서 privity 불요
⑤ HomeStore (retailer) 책임 — 유통망 전체 엄격책임
Issue 1: Manufacturing Defect (Alice v. LadderCo)
I
Was the ladder defective due to a manufacturing defect?
R
A manufacturing defect exists when a specific product departs from its intended design. The product is compared to the manufacturer's own specifications. Strict liability applies — no proof of fault required. Defect must have existed when the product left the manufacturer's control.
A
The standard design calls for 12-inch rung spacing. This ladder had 18-inch spacing due to a machine error — a clear departure from intended design. The defect existed when it left LadderCo's factory and caused Alice's fall.
C
LadderCo is strictly liable to Alice for the manufacturing defect.
📝 한국어 요점
제조 결함 = 개별 제품이 설계에서 이탈. 18인치 ≠ 표준 12인치 → 명백한 제조 결함. 과실 불요, strict liability.
Issue 2: Failure to Warn (Alice v. LadderCo)
I
Is LadderCo liable for failure to warn of known rung spacing risks?
R
A product is defective for failure to warn when the manufacturer knew or should have known of a risk and failed to provide adequate warning. Causation: plaintiff must show she would have read and heeded the warning. Note: a warning alone does NOT cure a design defect.
A
LadderCo's quality control team noted prior complaints about rung spacing — they had actual knowledge of the risk. No warning was issued. Alice, unaware of any risk, used the ladder in a normal manner. A warning would likely have altered her behavior.
C
LadderCo is additionally liable for failure to warn.
📝 한국어 요점
불만 인지 = 실제 지식 존재. 미경고 → failure to warn. Alice가 경고 읽었다면 행동 변경 가능 → causation 충족. 경고는 설계 결함을 치유하지 않음.
Issue 3: Tom's Claim (Bystander) — No Privity Required
I
Can Tom, who did not purchase the ladder, sue LadderCo and HomeStore under strict products liability?
R
Under modern strict products liability (Restatement §402A / §1), privity of contract is NOT required. Any foreseeable user or bystander injured by a defective product may bring a claim. The entire distribution chain (manufacturer + retailer) may be strictly liable.
A
Tom was a contractor working alongside Alice — a foreseeable bystander in the zone of danger of ladder use. He was struck by a dislodged rung. HomeStore, as a retailer in the distribution chain, is also strictly liable even though it did not manufacture the defect.
C
Tom may sue both LadderCo and HomeStore. Privity is no bar.
📝 한국어 요점
Privity 불요 — 구매자 아니어도 청구 가능. Tom = foreseeable bystander. HomeStore = 유통망 일원 → strict liability 공동 책임. Tom이 결함 제품에 직접 피해 = 청구 성립.
Essay 3 — Intentional Torts + Defenses (의도적 불법행위 + 방어)
Fact Pattern
Dave and Ed had an argument at a bar over a business deal gone wrong. As Dave turned to leave, Ed grabbed Dave's arm firmly and said, "You're not going anywhere until you give me my money back." Ed held Dave's arm for about three minutes. Dave has a rare nerve condition — unknown to Ed — that caused the firm grip to inflict permanent nerve damage to Dave's arm. Dave struggled to get free, stumbled, and hit his head on a barstool, suffering a concussion. Ed argues that Dave implicitly consented to the confrontation by engaging in the argument, and that he only meant to talk, not harm Dave.
Question: Discuss all intentional tort claims Dave may bring against Ed. Address Ed's defenses.
⚡ 쟁점 목록
① Battery — 팔 잡기, 바닥 쓰러짐 (intent, harmful contact)
② False Imprisonment — 3분간 구금 (confinement, no reasonable means of escape)
③ Consent Defense — 논쟁 참가 = 접촉 동의 여부 (scope of consent)
④ Eggshell Plaintiff — 신경 손상, Ed가 몰랐어도 배상 여부
⑤ Transferred Intent — 팔 잡기 의도 → 머리 부상까지 책임
Issue 1: Battery
I
Did Ed commit battery by grabbing Dave's arm?
R
Battery requires: (1) intentional act, (2) causing harmful or offensive contact with plaintiff's person. "Harmful" = physical injury. "Offensive" = would offend reasonable person's dignity. Intent to cause harm is NOT required — only intent to do the act.
A
Ed intentionally grabbed Dave's arm — the act was deliberate. The grip caused permanent nerve damage = harmful contact. Ed's stated intent ("only meant to talk") is irrelevant; he intended the grabbing act itself. As to the concussion: under transferred intent or proximate cause, Ed is liable for foreseeable consequences of the battery.
C
Ed committed battery. He is liable for both the nerve damage and the concussion.
📝 한국어 요점
Battery: 의도적으로 팔을 잡음 = 행위 의도 충족. 피해 의도 불요. 신경 손상 = harmful contact. 넘어져 머리 부상 → battery의 foreseeable 결과 → 전액 책임.
Issue 2: False Imprisonment
I
Did Ed falsely imprison Dave by holding his arm for three minutes?
R
False imprisonment: (1) intentional act that (2) confines plaintiff to a bounded area (3) without consent and (4) without reasonable means of escape. Physical force, threats, or assertion of authority all qualify. Duration need not be long.
A
Ed physically restrained Dave by holding his arm and verbally commanding him to stay ("you're not going anywhere"). Dave had no reasonable means of escape without risking further harm. Three minutes of confinement is sufficient. Dave did not consent to being physically restrained.
C
Ed is liable for false imprisonment.
📝 한국어 요점
False imprisonment: 신체적 구금 + "가지 마" 언어적 명령. 3분도 충분. 탈출 수단 없음. 동의 없음 → 성립. 기간 짧아도 무관.
Issue 3: Consent Defense (Ed's Defense)
I
Does Dave's participation in the argument constitute consent to physical contact?
R
Consent is a complete defense to intentional torts. However, consent only extends to the scope of what was actually agreed to. Implied consent from participating in an argument does NOT extend to physical restraint or harmful grabbing. Consent obtained through mistake or exceeded in scope is invalid.
A
Dave engaged in a verbal argument — this implies consent to verbal confrontation only. There is no basis to infer consent to physical grabbing or detention. Ed's physical act went beyond any implied consent arising from the argument.
C
Consent defense fails. Ed is liable.
📝 한국어 요점
Consent = 동의 범위 내에서만 유효. 말다툼 참여 = 언어적 대결 동의. 신체 구금까지 동의한 것 아님 → consent 방어 실패.
Issue 4: Eggshell Plaintiff (Nerve Damage)
I
Is Ed responsible for the permanent nerve damage even though he did not know of Dave's condition?
R
The eggshell plaintiff rule applies in intentional torts as well as negligence. A tortfeasor takes the victim as they find them. Unforeseeable extent of harm does not excuse the defendant.
A
Ed did not know of Dave's nerve condition. Nevertheless, once Ed committed battery, he is responsible for all resulting harm, including the unforeseeable permanent nerve damage. The type of harm (physical injury from grabbing) was foreseeable even if its extent was not.
C
Ed is liable for the full extent of Dave's nerve damage.
📝 한국어 요점
Eggshell rule = 의도적 불법행위에도 적용. 신경 질환 몰랐어도 관계없음. 피해 유형(접촉으로 인한 부상) 예견 가능했으면 충분 → 전액 배상.
📋 에세이 IRAC 템플릿 (암기)
I — Issue: "The issue is whether [party] is liable for [tort] under [theory]."
R — Rule: "[Tort] requires the plaintiff to prove (1)___ (2)___ (3)___."
A — Application: "Here, [apply facts to each element]. [Counter-argument + rebuttal if needed]."
C — Conclusion: "Therefore, [party] [is / is not] liable for [tort]."

자주 쓰는 문장:
· "The defendant will argue ___. However, this argument fails because ___."
· "Although ___, the [rule] does not require ___."
· "Even if ___, the plaintiff can still recover because ___."
· "This is a question of fact for the jury / law for the court."

Strict Liability 체크리스트: ① 결함 유형(제조/설계/경고) ② 결함이 통제 이탈 시 존재 ③ causation ④ damages → 과실(negligence) 언급 불요

Eggshell 쓰는 경우: 피고가 "예견 못했다" 항변 시 → "takes plaintiff as found, foreseeability of extent irrelevant"
